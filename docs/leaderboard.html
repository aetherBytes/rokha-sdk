The math — every weight, published

The score is one open formula. Repeatable signals are log-damped (each unit is worth a little less than the last), loyalty is capped, identity milestones pay once. Nothing else touches your rank.

score = Σ w·ln(1+x) repeatable signals + loyalty (bounded) + identity (one-time)

Damping in practice: your 1st rig run is worth ~243 points, your 100th ~3.5, your 1,000th ~0.35. Growth never stops — it just stays honest.

04

X engagement — weighted like X weighs it

We use everything we collect from your posts — views, likes, reposts, quotes and replies — and we don't treat them as equal. X open-sourced its ranking algorithm, so our engagement mix uses the same ratios its ranker uses to value interactions: a reply is a conversation, a like is a nod. Your "X engagement" number is the weighted sum below (then log-damped like every repeatable signal). Views are scored separately at weight 50.

InteractionUnits eachScaleWhy (per the open-source X algorithm)
↳Reply13.5
X's ranker scores a reply at 13.5 — conversation is the strongest signal there, so it is here. One reply = 27 likes.
❝Quote post1.5
A repost plus commentary — carries your work further than a bare repost
⇄Repost1.0
X's ranker baseline: retweet = 1.0
♥Like0.5
X's ranker values a fav at 0.5 — half a repost

Example: a post with 40 likes, 12 reposts, 2 quotes and 9 replies = 20 + 12 + 3 + 121.5 = 156.5 engagement units. Want to climb? Start conversations, not just impressions.

07

Fair by design

✓
Whales can't freeze the board.

Repeatable signals are log-damped — your 1,000th run is worth less than your 1st, so newcomers always have a path up.

✓
Shipped work rules the top.

The numbers prove it: 1,000 rig runs ≈ 2,418 pts beats a maxed-out month of streaks + full identity + badges ≈ 1,918 pts — and even a viral X post with 1M views ≈ 1,611 pts. Building wins, by design.

✓
Only your own X posts count.

Posts are verified against your linked account. Nobody can farm points with your link — or steal yours.

✓
Every score is auditable.

Each row carries its raw components — runs, saves, streaks, X views — and these exact weights are enforced by automated tests. What you read here is what runs.
